Detection Report

Cybersecurity Threat Analysis & Monitoring Summary

What is a Detection Report?

A Detection Report is a specialized document used primarily in cybersecurity, industrial inspections, and medical diagnostics. It acts as a formal record confirming that a specific anomaly, threat, or critical event has been identified by a monitoring system.

Core Components

Timestamp: Exact time when the threat was detected.

Source: Location such as IP address or system module.

Severity: Level of risk from Low to Critical.

Evidence: Logs, screenshots, or system traces.

Detection Workflow

The detection process follows a structured lifecycle: identification by automated tools, data collection for context, analysis to classify the threat, and reporting for decision-makers and compliance.

Why Detection Reports Matter

Detection reports provide accountability, ensure legal compliance, assist in root-cause analysis, and translate complex technical findings into understandable insights for stakeholders.
